Why is this important?

Quick Question

You are going on a trip to a far away country where you've never been. The flight ticket to get there cost you a lot, leaving you with limited spending money, and with only three days to visit. Which of the following can you do ahead of the trip to ensure you have the best time possible?

  • I'll do my research ahead of time (e.g. talk to people, read books, look online)

    Yes! How else would you know what to do there?

  • I'll prioritize what I want to see/do there ahead of time

    Definitely! This one is about being effective. Since you only have a limited time there, prioritizing will ensure you spend it on the most valuable things first.

  • I'll plan my schedule / itinerary ahead of time

    Right! This one is about being efficient: getting the most out of your limited resources (time, energy, money, etc.)

  • I'll just trust my luck when I get there.

    Probably not. In some cases you may end up having a lot of fun, but you risk missing out on things that are even more fun (or about as fun but less costly), or worse: ending up somewhere you don't want to be.

It's the same with your career

You have limited time, energy, money, and other resources, and you want to make the most of them career-wise.

You need to research your options, decide what's more important and/or more appealing to you, and come up with a plan to make it all happen.

Goals are how you know you've achieved your vision, and they have to be SMART:

  • Specific
  • Measurable
  • Ambitious
  • Reasonable
  • Time-based

Quick Question

How is the finish line a SMART goal? (Sort the table cells to match)

Specific There's only one finish line for the race, and it's very clear where it is.
Measurable The finish line is at exactly a certain distance from the starting line, and winning is determined by who finished the race in the shortest time.
Ambitious The finish line with its clock can tell you if you beat the other competitors or broke a record.
Reasonable The finish line (and indeed the entire race) is designed around the skill level of its participants. For example a marathon course (~26 miles) is not expected to be won in two minutes.
Time-based You must reach the finish line within a certain time, at least before they take it down and call it a day.

In order to help our Fellows focus on what matters most Braven conducted a pioneering national survey of diverse young professionals.

It revealed six key elements of a successful College Roadmap and what success looks like for each of these elements:

KEY ELEMENT WHY IT MATTERS WHAT HELPS ACCELERATE YOUR CAREER TIPS AND TRICKS FOR SUCCESS
GOOD GPA Good GPA is a requirement for top entry-level jobs ~3.6 average GPA amongst successful grads; all successful respondents reported GPA >3.0 For students with lower GPAs, strong academic experiences (e.g., study abroad) and summer internships are key to success, as are extracurricular involvement and leadership
PROFESSIONAL EXPOSURE AND INTERNSHIPS Internship experience in any field allows the opportunity to gain career skills, network, and better understand your professional interests (and may lead directly to a job offer). Vast majority of respondents had internships during summer before graduation (primarily to get professional reasons, not pay); industry of internship was less important. Involvement with professional societies or other school year professional experience also helpful There is no “right” way to find a summer or full-time job; most students use personal initiative (via college and personal connections, seeking jobs on their own) or on campus support (career services and on campus recruiting). More likely to yield a “two-for-one” internship + job (internship where student returns for full time job)
LEADERSHIP AND EXTRACURRICULAR ACTIVITIES It's an opportunity to develop your leadership, explore both professional and non-professional interests, and demonstrate your enthusiasm and abilities. Successful students join a range of extracurriculars, including service, career/ vocational, social, cultural and athletic orgs, serving in leadership roles ~50% of the time Investing in greater “depth” in becoming a leader of organizations most meaningful to you likely more valuable than being involved in many activities superficially
RELEVANCE AND RIGOR OF COURSE OF STUDY It can help you learn what interests you. Taking demanding courses in a specific area can demonstrate your commitment. Use academic pathway to explore interests and demonstrate interests; also ultimately valuable to have story on how academic pathway relevant to career interests There is no “right” major; pursuing “above and beyond” academic experiences like major projects or study abroad can help differentiate you
GETTING MENTORSHIP They can give advice and help you obtain opportunities (e.g., through introductions and references). Most successful students have multiple mentors, including professors / university staff / friends met through academics and extracurriculars 50% of most successful respondents used connection with senior exec or other influential person for advice or direct support getting a full time job
PEERS, SUPPORT GROUPS AND SOCIAL NETWORKS They can help push, advise, and support you.   Social friends, family and extracurricular friends most commonly cited support networks Motivated friends can help push you to achieve; friends with similar interests valuable for support, diff’t interests valuable to challenge your perspective
